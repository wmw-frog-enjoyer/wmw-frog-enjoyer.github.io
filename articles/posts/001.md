the more i play customs the more i realize the proportions stream slops have taken, it went from every other map, to almost every single map in the website. I have seen many different opinions in what is a stream slop, how to solve them, and many other things, and i wanted to share a bit of my perspective on them as someone who doesn't play as much as they used to, as well as sharing a quick little theory on why we see so many stream slops in sound space custom maps.

# what are stream slops?

i would first like to make clear what stream slops are, or at least the definition i will be using in this video. I have seen a lot of people who think that stream slops are any map with streams, and i have also seen a lot of people who would only qualify a stream slop a map that has a continuous stream when start to finish. I personally take a bit of a midpoint on that matter.

For me a stream slop is a map where all of the drops consist of only streams and those streams pose no variety across the map and similar ones (other stream slops) apart from its bpm, and i personally count not only streams maps but also wall maps.

# why do stream slops exist?

i wont say my theory on why the customs are infested with stream slops yet, but i will give a bit on an insight on the debate of why stream slops exist and rather they are good or bad for the community.

nowdays more then ever we saw an big influx of new players, nowdays the game is hitting around 250 players at peak hours, and a couple months ago we saw the game hit 400 players on peak hours, and as the community grows in general we start seeing more new mappers. A lot of the mappers we are used to have mapped for years, most mapping at least since 2023, when sound space plus was released, so they have a lot of experience mapping and their maps tend to be more polished. 

there is a topic about more experienced mappers leaning towards tech maps because they already mapped a lot of the more generic patterns but this isn't really the point im trying to make right now. 

with this new influx of mapper a lot of them came and started to map less polished things, copy and pasting a lot of the same pattern through the maps, simply flipping them or doing some very small changes so the map isn't fully the same, i have done the same thing in a lot of my own maps (though im not really a mapper, nor did i ever map stream slops), but i really cant blame them for simply flipping patterns here and there to make a map. 

The issue doesnt begin when mappers decide to map unpolished maps, this is normal, that is expected from beginner mappers, and the issue doesnt begin when those new mappers dont map tech or complex patterns, because again, they are beginners, they are not going to map a super complex tech map. The issue begins when the only pattern those mappers decide to map is the one with the least variety, and when there are so many of those maps that it becomes hard to find any other pattern other then streams.

streams are not diverse patterns, they can be clockwise, counter clockwise, vertical, horizontal, and thats pretty much it, at least for the most basic streams, there are screw jacks, and spins, and many other categories of streams but what we see, and what is easiest to map are only 4 variations of the pattern. If you take other patterns like jumps, there are super simple jumps, like star patterns, but a lot of them are a lot more unique, even though at the end of the day they are all just jumps. Im sure im missing a couple pattern categories on the mix here, again, im not really a mapper so i am not the best person to say a bunch of sub patterns of streams an jumps, but as a player i can confidently say that streams tend to be very similar across every single stream map, with the only major difference being the bpm.

# are stream slops good for the game?

beginner players tend to like streams more then jumps, thats because streams are a single string of notes that its easier to understand and follow. Beginner mappers tend to like mapping streams because after mapping a couple sections they can simply rotate copy and paste those patterns to finish their maps. not very active players tend to like simpler patterns, which usually include streams, as they are more familiar with those patterns and so they can play them without needing to grind and understand the pattern. So if people like playing them, and people like mapping them, are stream slops really an issue?

i would personally say that they are, for a couple of reasons, some more personal, and some more general.

1. mappers take longer to learn how to polish their mapping: when beginner mappers only map stream slops and the players play it a lot it creates incentives for the mapper to keep mapping the same thing, it creates a loop where they map streams over and over again which can create a comfort zone those mappers never want to leave. 

2. players take longer to learn new patterns: While the old players who arent very active dont really benefit from learning anything new, the new players do. Streams are easier to the new players, and it creates the same issue as the one with the mappers, where it creates a comfort zone which they dont want to leave, making it take longer for them to learn other patterns.

3. it hurts map discoverability: when most of the custom maps uploaded are stream slops it makes it harder for the players who want to get out of that comfort zone to do so, and while you might argue that you can simply filter out streams and filter in something like tech, not only you lose access to good tech maps that include tech streams, but also there still are stream slops that for some reason arent tagged as streams but are tagged as tech maps. It simply makes harder to find maps from other patterns.

4. good streams get buried: not all streams are stream slops, you can still have diversity within mainly stream maps, not only by making more unique streams, like quantum streams, but also by mixing in other patterns. A stream map is a map where its main pattern its streams, but that doesnt mean you cant mix jumps or slides or any other pattern on the middle to make the map more unique and fun. When there are so many stream slops it becomes harder to find good stream maps.

5. streams are boring both to play and to watch: when all that changes from one map to the other is its bpm it becomes very repetitive, and maybe the players who have been playing for a couple of months dont really find it repetitive but the ones who have played for years sure do (im not saying all players who played for years find them repetitive but they do have a tendency of disliking stream slops).

6. Stream slops can create a false perception of what good mapping is: When a new mapper joins the community and browses through custom maps, all they see are stream slops. Naturally, they are going to assume that those maps represent what good mapping looks like. This isn't really about mappers refusing to leave their comfort zone, but rather about them not knowing any better. New mappers learn by looking at what other people make, and if most of what they see are repetitive stream maps, they are more likely to copy those same ideas. This creates a cycle where stream slops encourage even more stream slops, making it harder for new mappers to discover what polished and varied mapping can actually look like.

# how did stream slops came to be?

this is just a theory on why out of all patterns beginner mappers could pick from, they picked streams. I the problem starts between the release of rhythia online and steam rhythia. When rhythia online was first on, all the good maps to farm rp were legacy maps, there weren't a lot of ranked maps, and the ones that were didn't give much rp. But as the game evolved and more maps were ranked and the rp algorithm changed the system started showing a very clear bias towards stream maps, where stream maps give significantly more rp and any other pattern on the game. 

My personal skillset for maps focuses primarily in jumps, but i am also good at techs, and quantum maps, my skillset is what many people would consider and the furthest thing from stream slops, and yet i gain way more rp by playing streams than any other pattern (that said i haven't played cunevs game in months).

when players realize that stream maps give more rp they nature start playing more stream maps, and when mappers realize players are playing more stream maps, they naturally start mapping more streams, and when sound spaces and rhythias community overlap so much it is only natural that the mappers that map on rhythia also map on sound space, which explain why we see so many streams slops on sound space customs.

# conclusion

At the end of the day, I don't think streams are inherently bad. Some of my favorite maps are stream maps, and streams have their place in the game. The issue isn't that stream maps exist, it's that they have become so dominant that they are starting to crowd out everything else.

When players mostly play streams, mappers mostly map streams. When mappers mostly map streams, new players and mappers start to think that streams are all there is to the game. This creates a cycle where the same type of map keeps getting made over and over again, making it harder for other patterns and mapping styles to grow.

Maybe my theory on why stream slops became so common is completely wrong, but one thing is clear: if you browse customs today, you'll find far more stream slops than you would have a couple of years ago. Whether you think that's a good thing or a bad thing is up to you, but personally, I would rather see a customs page filled with a variety of patterns than one where every other map is just another stream map with a different BPM.
